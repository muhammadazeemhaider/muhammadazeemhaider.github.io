    <h2>About Me</h2>

    <p>
      Hi, I'm Muhammad Azeem Haider. I am a graduate student at
      <a href="https://www.cics.umass.edu/">Manning College for Information and Computer Science</a>
      at University of Massachusetts Amherst. Previously, I graduated from
      <a href="https://habib.edu.pk">Habib University</a> in 2024 with a Bachelors of Science in
      Computer Science and a minor in Electrical and Computer Engineering.
      After graduation, I worked as a Machine Learning Engineer in the
      telecommunications industry, where I worked on developing analytical NLP
      solutions for internal and external stakeholders.
    </p>

    <p>
      I am fascinated by how the brain works and want to understand how it
      functions the way it does, as well as how we might replicate the language
      and cognitive abilities in language models that come so naturally to
      humans. I am also interested in working with retrieval systems,
      understanding how retrieval can be improved within generative pipelines,
      and exploring how sparse retrieval methods can be made more effective and
      efficient as standalone systems.
    </p>
  </div>
